# Executive Summary
P-glycoprotein (P-gp) inhibition correlates with large, hydrophobic, and polyfunctional molecules. Inhibitors tend to exceed typical “druglike” limits (high molecular weight, lipophilicity) and often feature multiple aromatic and basic centers. Conversely, small, highly polar or flexible compounds with many H-bond donors are unlikely to inhibit P-gp. Below are succinct structural/physicochemical rules (positive or negative) for predicting P-gp inhibition, distilled from experimental and computational studies.

## Key Rules for P-gp Inhibition
1. **High Molecular Weight** (MW > 500) – increases likelihood.
2. **Moderate MW Threshold** (MW ≥ 300) – tends to favor inhibition.
3. **Low MW** (MW < 300) – decreases likelihood.
4. **High Lipophilicity** (cLogP ≥ 3) – increases likelihood.
5. **Low Lipophilicity** (cLogP ≤ 3) – decreases likelihood.
6. **High Polar Surface Area** (TPSA > 75 Å²) – increases P-gp engagement.
7. **Low Polar Surface Area** (TPSA ≤ 75 Å²) – decreases engagement.
8. **H-Bond Acceptors ≥ 3** – increases likelihood.
9. **H-Bond Acceptors ≤ 2** – decreases likelihood.
10. **H-Bond Donors ≤ 3** – increases likelihood (fewer donors).
11. **H-Bond Donors ≥ 4** – decreases likelihood.
12. **Polar Heteroatoms (N+O ≥ 4)** – increases likelihood.
13. **Polar Heteroatoms (N+O ≤ 3)** – decreases likelihood.
14. **Basic (Cationic) Center** (e.g. tertiary amine, base pKa > ~8) – increases likelihood.
15. **Weak Base** (base pKa < 8) – decreases likelihood.
16. **Aromatic Rings (≥2, planar)** – increases likelihood.
17. **Aromatic Linkers (vs aliphatic)** – increases likelihood.
18. **Bulky Hydrophobic Moieties** (e.g. adamantane or long alkyl/aromatic groups) – increases likelihood.
