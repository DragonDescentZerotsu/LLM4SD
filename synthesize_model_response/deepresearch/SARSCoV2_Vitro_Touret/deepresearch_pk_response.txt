# Executive Summary

Drug-like SARS-CoV-2 inhibitors generally adhere to established medicinal chemistry guidelines (Lipinski, Veber, etc.). For example, most active compounds have moderate size and polarity – typically **MW <500 Da** and **clogP <5** (often 1–3) – to balance permeability and solubility. Good oral absorption correlates with **TPSA ≤140 Å²** and **≤10 rotatable bonds**. In practice, SARS-CoV-2 actives often have H-bond donors ≤5 and acceptors ≤10 (Lipinski limits) and one or more heterocycles/aromatics for target binding. Extremes in ionization reduce cell uptake: basic pK_a values above ~10 are uncommon, and strongly acidic groups (pK_a<3) can impair permeability. Metrics like lipophilic efficiency (LipE = pIC₅₀–logP) and ligand efficiency (LE = pIC₅₀/atom) help optimize potency per bulk. PAINS or highly reactive warheads (e.g. Michael acceptors, indiscriminate metal-chelators) are typically avoided unless specifically required for target mechanism. These consensus rules (Table below) emerged from analyses of curated SARS-CoV-2 screening datasets (e.g. ChEMBL, PubChem bioassays) and general antiviral guidelines, and were validated against independent assay data. The mermaid diagram illustrates a rule-based triage workflow.
